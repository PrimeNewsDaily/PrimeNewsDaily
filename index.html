Top 10 Video Games Set in Italy That Will Make You Want a Visa

Have you ever played a video game and thought, “Wait, where is this place? Can I actually go there?” I’ve had that moment more times than I can count, especially with games set in Italy. Whether it’s wandering through ancient Roman ruins, zipping past Tuscan vineyards on a motorcycle, or leaping across Venetian rooftops, there’s something about virtual Italy that makes you want to pack your bags and look for an Italy visa consultant.

But the real question is: Why do these games make us so obsessed with the idea of visiting the real thing?

Why Do Games Set in Italy Leave Such a Lasting Impact?

There’s more to it than just pretty views. Italy isn’t just a backdrop; it’s practically a character in these stories. From the textured walls of Renaissance Florence to the narrow alleys of Naples, the details are what pull you in. Playing through these digital worlds feels like free travel therapy. You don’t just see Italy you live it, even if only for a few hours at a time.

And that’s not all. Games have this rare power to connect history, culture, food, art, and architecture in ways textbooks never could. When you visit these places later in real life, there’s this surreal moment of déjà vu. It’s like walking into a memory from your console.

How Are These Games So Accurate About Italy?

It’s not just random. Developers do deep research. They consult historical records, use real maps, scan architectural structures, and often visit the cities themselves. That’s why you’ll find actual landmarks like the Colosseum in Rome or the canals of Venice rendered with stunning precision. These games don’t just entertain they educate, immerse, and in many cases, inspire travel.

Let’s break down the top 10 games that turned my screen into a window to Italy.

1. Assassin’s Creed II What’s It Like to Climb Florence’s Duomo?

When you think of Italy in video games, this one’s probably at the top. Assassin’s Creed II lets you play as Ezio Auditore, a Florentine noble-turned-assassin during the Italian Renaissance. The game is a cultural treasure chest. Florence, Venice, San Gimignano, and Forlì are recreated with breathtaking detail.

  • Architectural landmarks like the Basilica di Santa Maria del Fiore are climbable
  • Street layouts closely match their real-life counterparts
  • Historical figures like Leonardo da Vinci play major roles

The textures of crumbling bricks, the bell sounds echoing through narrow alleys, and Renaissance fashion breathe authenticity into the experience. No wonder so many players find themselves Googling flights to Tuscany afterward.

2. Gran Turismo 7 Is Driving Through Tuscany as Beautiful as It Looks?

Gran Turismo 7 isn’t just about high-speed races. In its European circuits, you can drive through regions inspired by the Tuscan countryside and the Amalfi Coast. The lighting, scenery, and winding roads feel almost too beautiful to be real.

  • Morning fog lifting over rolling hills
  • Cypress trees dotting ancient roads
  • Coastal drives lined with cliff-hugging villas

3. Rome Total War Can You Actually Learn History From a Strategy Game?

This title pulls you straight into the political and military machinations of ancient Rome. From the Roman Senate to famous generals like Scipio Africanus, the game is rooted in legitimate historical dynamics.

  • City layouts based on archaeological sources
  • Battle tactics that reflect actual Roman legions
  • Cultural units like velites, hastati, and gladiators

4. Hitman 2 What’s It Like Assassinating Targets at an Italian Beach Town?

In the Sapienza mission of Hitman 2, you’re dropped into a fictional town on the Amalfi Coast. The vibe is rich narrow lanes, seaside cafés, buzzing mopeds, and pastel houses stacked against cliffs.

  • Positano’s vertical design and layout
  • Local dialects and background sounds
  • Regional dishes on NPC tables, like spaghetti alle vongole

5. Mafia II How Does Sicily Shape a Gangster’s Origin?

The game opens in Sicily during World War II. Bombed-out towns, cobblestone roads, and traditional Sicilian villas set the stage for Vito Scaletta’s early years before moving to Empire Bay (a fictional New York).

  • Olive groves and family estates
  • Towns modeled after Palermo and Corleone
  • Authentic dialogue in Sicilian dialect

6. Assassin’s Creed Brotherhood Is Rome More Than Just the Colosseum?

This installment expands on Assassin’s Creed II and focuses entirely on Rome. The attention to urban sprawl is astonishing. You can walk from Vatican City to the Colosseum and notice realistic transitions in architecture and wealth.

  • The Pantheon, Sistine Chapel, and Castel Sant'Angelo are explorable
  • Roman aqueducts function as transport paths
  • Street vendors sell items based on 16th-century commerce

7. The Saboteur What’s Paris Doing Here? Wait, There's Italian Influence?

Although the main story happens in Nazi-occupied Paris, parts of the game involve the Italian resistance. The game dives into the complex relationship between Italy and Germany during WWII, touching on political tensions, betrayal, and underground movements.

  • Black market arms networks routed through Northern Italy
  • Vehicles and uniforms inspired by Italian Fascist styles
  • Italian operatives with realistic dialogue and regional accents

8. Forza Horizon 2 How Does It Feel to Race Through Italian Wine Country?

Set in a fictional southern European region heavily inspired by Liguria and Tuscany, Forza Horizon 2 is all about open-world driving. You’ll cruise through vineyards, past medieval towns, and down sun-kissed roads.

  • Day/night cycles and weather effects mirror Italian seasons
  • Festival culture modeled after real Italian celebrations
  • Town names, signage, and music inspired by local life

9. Shadow of Rome Does Gladiator Life in Ancient Italy Still Fascinate Us?

This lesser-known gem mixes third-person combat with stealth missions in ancient Rome. It's brutal, dramatic, and deeply historical. You step into the shoes of Agrippa, a Roman general turned gladiator.

  • Coliseum mechanics based on actual gladiatorial events
  • Civilian areas recreate Roman class structures
  • Weaponry and armor modeled after museum references

10. Assassin’s Creed Revelations (Desmond’s Sequences) Is Italy Still Home in a Sci-fi World?

While much of this game is set in Constantinople, Desmond’s modern-day sequences bring us back to Monteriggioni in Italy. The villa and town offer a calm, lived-in feeling that contrasts beautifully with the chaos of the game’s main missions.

  • Reflections of real-life Tuscan villa layouts
  • Family crypts and libraries based on Renaissance blueprints
  • Background sounds like church bells and chirping cicadas
